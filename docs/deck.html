not a classifier — an adjudicator

A grounded verdict with issue, part, severity, risk flags, supporting image IDs and a justification — and it abstains (needs-info → human) when it can't see enough. The image is the source of truth, never the user's words.

09 · two product shapes
B2C agent · B2B layer

An agent for your claims — or for your queue.

B2C — "handles all your claims"

Snap a photo in chat; the agent verifies it live and returns a grounded verdict, escalating when it can't see enough. (The PWA today.)

B2B — automate the clear-cut

Drop into existing helpdesk/claims flows: auto-clear the unambiguous, escalate the rest to a human. "Uber for claim review."

target: industry deflection is 65–80% for high-structure intents — a goal for our task, not a measured ClaimLens result yet.

10 · the playbook
proven at scale elsewhere — bring it to insurance

Uber & Zomato already verify evidence at scale.

food delivery"arrived spilled / wrong item" → photo-verified refunds; rider & restaurant onboarding docs
insurance ▸same pattern, higher stakes — verify the damage photo against the claim, auto-clear the clear-cut, escalate the rest
why automate it · standard benefits

Instant triage

Seconds, not a queue — decisions at claim time, 24/7.

Lower cost / claim

Cut manual-review effort; scale with volume, not headcount.

Consistent & auditable

Same rubric every time, with a grounded trail for disputes.

Fraud & leakage ↓

Catch recycled / edited / wrong-object photos humans miss.

Scales 24/7

Absorb spikes without backlog — no fatigue, no drift.

Humans on hard cases

Adjusters spend time where judgment matters, not triage.
